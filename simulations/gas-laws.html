Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT

Relates pressure (P), volume (V), number of moles (n), temperature (T), and the gas constant (R = 0.0821 L.atm.mol-1.K-1).

Boyle's Law

P1V1 = P2V2

At constant temperature and moles, pressure and volume are inversely proportional. Squeeze the gas (decrease V) and pressure goes up (increase P).

Charles's Law

V1 / T1 = V2 / T2

At constant pressure and moles, volume is proportional to temperature. Heat the gas (increase T) and it expands (increase V).

Gay-Lussac's Law

P1 / T1 = P2 / T2

At constant volume and moles, pressure is proportional to temperature. Heat the gas (increase T) and pressure rises (increase P).

Avogadro's Law

V1 / n1 = V2 / n2

At constant pressure and temperature, volume is proportional to the number of moles. More particles (increase n) take up more space (increase V).
